Investment Indicators

Definition: Investment indicators provide insights into the allocation of resources towards the production of goods and services in the future, and are essential for understanding economic growth and productivity.

Importance:

Investment indicators are crucial for understanding economic growth, productivity, and future economic potential. They influence policy decisions and investment strategies.

Gross Capital Formation (Annual Growth %)

Definition: Gross capital formation, also known as gross domestic investment, measures the annual increase in physical assets such as buildings, machinery, and equipment.

Foreign Direct Investment (FDI), Net Inflows (% of GDP)

Definition: Foreign Direct Investment (FDI) net inflows refer to the value of inward direct investment made by non-resident investors in the reporting economy, expressed as a percentage of GDP.

Gross Fixed Capital Formation (% of GDP)

Definition: Gross fixed capital formation measures the value of acquisitions of new or existing fixed assets by the business sector, governments, and households (less disposals) as a percentage of GDP.
