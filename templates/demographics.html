Demographics

Definition: Demographics refer to statistical data relating to the population and particular groups within it. These statistics provide insights into the population structure, growth, distribution, and trends, which are crucial for planning and policy-making.

Importance:

Understanding demographic trends is essential for government planning, economic forecasting, and social services. It helps in the allocation of resources, development of policies, and implementation of programs targeting specific population groups.

Population Density (People per sq km)

Definition: Population density measures the number of people living per square kilometer of land area. It provides an indication of how crowded or sparsely populated an area is.

Urban Population (% of Total Population)

Definition: Urban population refers to the percentage of the total population living in urban areas. This metric highlights the degree of urbanization in a country.

Population by Age Group

Definition: Population by age group divides the total population into different age categories, such as 0-14 years, 15-64 years, and 65 years and above. It provides insights into the age structure of the population.
