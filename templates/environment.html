Environmental Indicators

Definition: Environmental indicators are metrics used to measure the state of the environment, track changes over time, and assess the impact of human activities on natural resources and ecosystems.

Importance:

Environmental indicators help policymakers, researchers, and the public understand the health of the environment. They are essential for monitoring environmental quality, informing policy decisions, and promoting sustainable development.

CO2 Emissions (kt)

Definition: CO2 emissions refer to the release of carbon dioxide into the atmosphere from burning fossil fuels, industrial processes, and other sources. It is measured in kilotons (kt).

Renewable Internal Freshwater Resources (per capita)

Definition: Renewable internal freshwater resources refer to the total volume of internal freshwater generated through the water cycle, divided by the population. It is measured in cubic meters per capita.

Explanation: The bar chart below illustrates the availability of freshwater resources per capita. This visualization helps to assess water availability and stress levels in the population.

Protected Areas (% of Total Territorial Area)

Definition: Protected areas are regions designated for the conservation of nature and biodiversity, expressed as a percentage of the total territorial area.

Explanation: The bar chart below shows the percentage of land and marine areas protected over the years. This visualization helps to understand the efforts and progress in conservation and biodiversity protection.

Air Pollution (PM2.5)

Definition: PM2.5 refers to fine particulate matter with a diameter of less than 2.5 micrometers. It is a significant indicator of air pollution and poses health risks when present at high levels.
