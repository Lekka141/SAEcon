Poverty and Inequality Indicators

Definition: Poverty and inequality indicators provide insights into the distribution of wealth and the economic well-being of a population.

Importance:

Poverty and inequality metrics are crucial for understanding the economic disparities within a country. They help in formulating policies to reduce poverty and improve income distribution.

Gini Index

Definition: The Gini Index is a measure of income inequality within a population, ranging from 0 (perfect equality) to 100 (perfect inequality).

Poverty Headcount Ratio at $2.15 a Day (2017 PPP) (% of Population)

Definition: This metric measures the percentage of the population living on less than $2.15 a day at 2017 international prices, adjusted for purchasing power parity (PPP).
